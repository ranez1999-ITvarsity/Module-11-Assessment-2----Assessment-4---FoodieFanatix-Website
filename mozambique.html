MOZAMBIQUE

    Location
  • Mozambique is located on the southeastern coast of Africa, bordered by the Indian Ocean to the east.

    • History
  • Mozambique’s history is rich and diverse, shaped by African, Arab, and European influences over centuries. Indigenous communities were the first inhabitants, thriving through agriculture and trade. By the 8th century, Arab traders began establishing settlements along the coastline, integrating the region into the broader Indian Ocean trade network. The Portuguese arrived in the early 16th century, marking the beginning of nearly 500 years of colonial rule. Mozambique gained independence in 1975, though it quickly faced a long and challenging civil war that lasted until 1992. Today, Mozambique is building its economy and strengthening its cultural identity, emerging as a key player in Southern Africa.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • Bazaruto Archipelago: Known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean," this group of islands boasts crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and abundant marine life, making it a prime location for snorkeling, diving, and fishing.

  • Fun Fact
    A fun fact about Mozambique is that it’s one of the only countries in the world to feature a weapon on its national flag!

Mozambiques Flavours

Mozambique’s culinary history is a rich blend of indigenous African traditions and Portuguese, Arab, and Indian influences, a result of centuries of trade and colonization. Staple foods like cassava, maize, and millet are central to traditional dishes, while the coast provides abundant seafood such as prawns, a key ingredient in many meals. The Portuguese introduced ingredients like chili, garlic, onions, and peanuts, which are now integral to Mozambican cuisine. Dishes like piri-piri chicken, matapa (cassava leaves in peanut sauce), and preto (a type of shrimp stew) reflect this diverse fusion of flavors

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in Mozambique

PERÍ PERÍ CHICKEN

Pírí pírí chicken is a Mozambican dish consisting of grilled or roasted chicken that is often served with a creamy and spicy coconut sauce. Before grilling, the meat is traditionally marinated in cumin, garlic, paprika, lemon juice, and bird's eye chilis, giving the chicken a unique flavor. The name of the dish is a Portuguese transliteration of the Swahili phrase pírí pírí, meaning pepper pepper, referring to the spiciness of the dish
