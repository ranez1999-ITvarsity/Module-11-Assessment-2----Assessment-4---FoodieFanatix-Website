PUERTO RICO

    Location
  • Puerto Rico is an island located in the northeastern Caribbean Sea, east of the Dominican Republic and west of the Virgin Islands. It’s a U.S. territory, positioned between the Caribbean Sea and the North Atlantic Ocean.

    • History
  • Puerto Rico’s history is a fascinating blend of Indigenous, Spanish, and American influences. The island was originally inhabited by the Taíno people until it was claimed by Christopher Columbus for Spain in 1493. Puerto Rico remained under Spanish rule for over 400 years, during which it developed a unique cultural identity, blending Taíno, African, and Spanish traditions. In 1898, as a result of the Spanish-American War, Puerto Rico was ceded to the United States, marking the beginning of a new era in its history. In 1917, Puerto Ricans were granted U.S. citizenship, and in 1952, Puerto Rico became a self-governing U.S. Commonwealth. Today, the island maintains a vibrant cultural heritage while navigating its political relationship with the United States.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • The number one tourist attraction in Puerto Rico is Old San Juan, a historic district with colorful colonial architecture, cobblestone streets, and a rich cultural atmosphere. Visitors to Old San Juan can explore iconic landmarks like Castillo San Felipe del Morro and Castillo San Cristóbal, which are centuries-old Spanish forts overlooking the ocean. The area is filled with museums, restaurants, art galleries, and shops, making it a lively hub of Puerto Rican history and culture.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about Puerto Rico is that it is home to the only tropical rainforest in the U.S. National Forest System—El Yunque National Forest. Covering over 28,000 acres, El Yunque is known for its lush vegetation, scenic waterfalls, and diverse wildlife, including the famous coquí frog, which is native to the island and often heard at night.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in Puerto Rico

PERNIL

Pernil is one of Puerto Rico's most famous dishes, a succulent roasted lamb shoulder that is traditionally seasoned in a marinade called adobo mojado, consisting of paprika, salt, vinegar, garlic, and oregano. The name of the dish is derived from the Spanish word pierna, meaning leg, but it is also a Catalan word for lamb, referring to the traditional recipe that calls for fresh lamb. Because lamb shoulder is more available and costs less than lamb, it has become a key ingredient in pernil. Many people prefer it since it is believed to be much more flavorful than lamb. The dish is a staple at numerous Puerto Rican festivities such as birthdays or weddings, where many people feast on the crunchy skin and tender meat that falls off the bone.
