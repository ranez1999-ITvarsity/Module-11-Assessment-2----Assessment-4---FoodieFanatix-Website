FRENCH POLYNESIA

    Location
  • French Polynesia is located in the South Pacific Ocean, about halfway between California and Australia. It is a vast collection of over 100 islands and atolls spread across 2,000 kilometers (1,242 miles), with Tahiti being its largest and most well-known island.

    • History
  • French Polynesia has a rich and layered history that spans thousands of years. Initially settled by Polynesian voyagers around 2000 BCE, the islands developed unique cultural practices, languages, and social structures. European explorers arrived in the 18th century, with James Cook’s expeditions bringing global attention to the islands. In 1842, France declared Tahiti and the Marquesas Islands a protectorate, and by 1880, they were officially annexed as French colonies. In 1957, the region was designated as an overseas territory of France, and in 2004 it gained more autonomy as an overseas collectivity, allowing it to have more control over its local governance while still remaining part of France.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • The number one tourist attraction in French Polynesia is Bora Bora. Known as the "Pearl of the Pacific," Bora Bora is famous for its turquoise lagoons, overwater bungalows, coral reefs, and the dramatic silhouette of Mount Otemanu at its center. The island is a world-renowned destination for honeymooners, luxury travelers, and nature enthusiasts who seek breathtaking natural beauty, pristine beaches, and a tranquil atmosphere.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about French Polynesia is that it is one of the world’s premier destinations for black pearls. The lagoons, especially in the Tuamotu Archipelago, are ideal for cultivating these unique pearls, which are highly prized for their dark hues and shimmering luster. Black pearls are a significant part of the local economy and a popular souvenir for visitors.

French Polynesias Flavours

The cuisine of French Polynesia is a delightful fusion of Polynesian and French influences. Poisson cru (raw fish marinated in coconut milk and lime juice) is one of the most beloved local dishes. Other staples include tropical fruits like coconut, papaya, and pineapple, as well as freshly caught seafood, often prepared with vanilla, which is also grown in the region. French culinary techniques combine with local ingredients to create dishes like po’e (a banana or papaya pudding) and coconut milk-infused preparations, reflecting both Polynesian heritage and French refinement.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in French Polynesia

POISSON CRU

Poisson cru is a Tahitian national dish that consists of raw tuna, lime juice, various vegetables, and coconut milk. It is also known as ia ota or e'ia ota, which means raw fish. The dish is prepared by briefly marinating tuna in lime juice, and then coconut milk is added to mellow its acidity. For a more authentic dish, fresh coconut meat is wrapped in cloth, and the milky juice is then squeezed over the fish and vegetables. Poisson cru is sweet, refreshing, and exotic, and can be found almost anywhere in Tahiti.
