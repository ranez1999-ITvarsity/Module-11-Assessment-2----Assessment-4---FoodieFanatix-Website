My Favourites

The Bahamas

Bahamian cuisine is a delicious fusion of African, Caribbean, and European influences, reflecting the islands' rich cultural history. The cuisine is centered around fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and a variety of spices. One of the most famous dishes is conch, a sea snail that’s served in many forms, including conch salad, cracked conch (battered and fried), and conch fritters. Rock lobster and snapper are other popular seafood choices, often grilled or stewed with spices, herbs, and vegetables.

Fiji

The number one tourist attraction in Fiji is the Mamanuca Islands, a picturesque archipelago of 20 islands known for their crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and luxury resorts. Popular for snorkeling, diving, and beach relaxation, the Mamanuca Islands are a tropical paradise. Accessible from the main island of Viti Levu, these islands attract visitors with stunning beaches, vibrant marine life, and activities ranging from water sports to cultural experiences in local Fijian villages.

Greece

Greece's cultural food history is a rich tapestry woven from ancient Mediterranean traditions and centuries of regional influences. Rooted in ingredients like olive oil, wheat, and wine, Greek cuisine reflects its agricultural heritage. Ancient Greeks emphasized simplicity and balance in their diet, with foods like bread, olives, and figs being central. Over time, Byzantine, Ottoman, and Venetian influences introduced new flavours such as spices, yogurt, and phyllo pastry. Classic dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, and spanakopita highlight this blend of ancient practices with later influences, creating a cuisine that celebrates fresh ingredients and bold flavours.
