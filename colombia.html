COLOMBIA

    Location
  • Colombia is located in the northwest corner of South America, bordered by Venezuela to the east, Brazil to the southeast, Peru to the south, and Panama to the northwest.

    • History
  • Colombia’s history is rich and diverse, shaped by indigenous cultures, Spanish colonization, and modern independence. Before European contact, Colombia was home to various indigenous groups, including the Muisca, Tairona, and Quimbaya, who built complex societies with distinct traditions. In 1499, Spanish explorers arrived, marking the beginning of colonial rule that lasted for over 300 years. Colombia gained independence from Spain in 1819, led by Simón Bolívar, who envisioned a united Gran Colombia, encompassing Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, and Panama. Although Gran Colombia dissolved in 1831, Colombia continued to develop its unique national identity. The country faced periods of political instability, including the Thousand Days’ War and the conflict with drug cartels in the late 20th century. Today, Colombia is a thriving, culturally rich democracy, known for its resilience, biodiversity, and vibrant culture.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • The number one tourist attraction in Colombia is Cartagena’s Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage site with well-preserved colonial architecture, charming cobblestone streets, and vibrant plazas. Visitors are drawn to its historic fortifications, colorful buildings, and lively atmosphere, along with views of the Caribbean Sea. Cartagena offers a unique blend of history, culture, and tropical beauty, making it one of Colombia’s most popular destinations.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about Colombia is that it is one of the world’s largest producers of emeralds! Colombian emeralds are prized for their quality, vibrant color, and clarity, making them some of the most sought-after in the global jewelry market. The country’s mining regions, especially in Boyacá and Cundinamarca, are known for producing these valuable gemstones.

Colombia’s Flavours

Colombian cuisine is a fusion of indigenous, African, and Spanish influences, offering a wide range of flavors and ingredients. The cuisine varies greatly by region, but staples include corn, potatoes, and rice. Popular dishes are arepas (corn cakes), bandeja paisa (a hearty platter with beans, rice, meat, and plantains), and ajiaco (a chicken and potato soup with corn). Coastal regions are known for fresh seafood, often flavored with coconut milk and spices. Colombia’s cuisine is rich, diverse, and reflective of its cultural heritage and agricultural bounty.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in Colombia

BANDEJA PAISA

Bandeja paisa, also known as bandeja de arriero, bandeja montanera and bandeja antioquena, is Colombia's national dish since 2005, a heavy, caloric meal traditionally served on a big, oval platter and intended to be eaten for lunch. Originally, the platter provided nutrition and energy to the farmers to keep them going for the whole day and there are a total of 13 ingredients used in the dish, including golden-fried chorizo sausages with lime, hogau sauce, cooked white rice, ground beef, plantain, arepa (a thin, fried corn cake), avocado, stewed red beans, and a fried egg on top of it all
