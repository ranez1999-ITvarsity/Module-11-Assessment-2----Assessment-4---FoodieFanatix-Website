GREECE

    Location
  • Greece is located in southeastern Europe, situated at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

    • History
  • Greece has a rich and storied history that dates back thousands of years, often considered the cradle of Western civilization. Ancient Greece was home to the first democratic government, developed early forms of philosophy, art, and science, and gave rise to influential city-states such as Athens and Sparta. The period of Classical Greece (5th to 4th century BCE) saw remarkable achievements in literature, theater, and architecture, exemplified by the works of playwrights like Sophocles and philosophers like Socrates and Plato. After the conquests of Alexander the Great in the 4th century BCE, Greek culture spread across a vast empire, influencing regions from the Mediterranean to Asia. Following centuries of Roman rule, Greece became part of the Byzantine Empire and later fell to the Ottoman Turks in the 15th century. Greece gained independence in the 19th century and has since evolved into a modern nation-state, known for its vibrant culture and historical heritage.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • Greece's number one tourist attraction is the Acropolis of Athens, a UNESCO World Heritage site that features ancient ruins, including the iconic Parthenon temple. The Acropolis, perched on a rocky outcrop overlooking the city, is a symbol of ancient Greek civilization and democracy. Visitors flock to the Acropolis to explore its historical significance, stunning architecture, and breathtaking views of Athens.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about Greece is that it has an astonishing number of islands—around 6,000 in total, although only about 227 are inhabited. These islands vary greatly in size, character, and culture, offering diverse experiences for travelers. From the picturesque beaches of Santorini and Mykonos to the lush landscapes of Corfu and the historical sites of Crete, Greece's islands are renowned for their beauty and charm, making the country a popular destination for island-hopping adventures.

Greece Flavours

Greece's cultural food history is a rich tapestry woven from ancient Mediterranean traditions and centuries of regional influences. Rooted in ingredients like olive oil, wheat, and wine, Greek cuisine reflects its agricultural heritage. Ancient Greeks emphasized simplicity and balance in their diet, with foods like bread, olives, and figs being central. Over time, Byzantine, Ottoman, and Venetian influences introduced new flavours such as spices, yogurt, and phyllo pastry. Classic dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, and spanakopita highlight this blend of ancient practices with later influences, creating a cuisine that celebrates fresh ingredients and bold flavours.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in Greece

SPANAKOPITA

Spanakopita is a Greek spinach pie consisting of a buttery, flaky phyllo pastry with a filling of cooked spinach, lemon juice, feta cheese, and sometimes dill. It can be served either as a small snack, an appetizer, or a light main course. Due to a farming tradition of handheld foods, the pies were originally invented for the field workers who would carry them in their pockets and consume them while working.
