THE BAHAMAS

    Location
  • The Bahamas is an archipelago located in the Atlantic Ocean, southeast of Florida and northeast of Cuba.

    • History
  • The Bahamas has a rich and complex history that dates back to 300–400 AD, when the first Indigenous people, the Lucayans, arrived from South America. In 1492, Christopher Columbus made landfall in The Bahamas, marking the beginning of European contact. Over the next centuries, the islands were claimed by the Spanish and later became a haven for pirates, including infamous figures like Blackbeard. In the 18th century, The Bahamas became a British colony, attracting settlers and developing industries such as salt and agriculture. The islands played a significant role in the Transatlantic Slave Trade, and many enslaved Africans were brought to work there. After slavery was abolished, the descendants of freed Africans formed the majority of the population. The Bahamas gained independence from Britain on July 10, 1973, but remains part of the Commonwealth, with a parliamentary government and a Governor-General representing the British monarch.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • The Bahamas' most famous tourist attraction is the Exuma Cays and the swimming pigs of Pig Beach. This unique destination offers crystal-clear turquoise waters, secluded beaches, and a remarkable opportunity to interact with the friendly pigs that swim freely in the shallow waters. It’s a one-of-a-kind experience that draws visitors from all over the world. The Exumas also offer world-class snorkeling and diving, along with luxury resorts and stunning natural beauty, making it a must-visit spot in the islands.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about The Bahamas is that it was home to the world's first-ever land-and-sea national park, the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park, established in 1958. This park is known for its pristine coral reefs, vibrant marine life, and crystal-clear waters, providing a safe habitat for species like the endangered Bahama parrot and the Caribbean reef shark. The Bahamas is also one of the most popular locations in the world for underwater filming, thanks to its remarkable visibility and diverse marine ecosystems.

The Bahamas Flavours

Bahamanian cuisine is a delicious fusion of African, Caribbean, and European influences, reflecting the islands' rich cultural history. The cuisine is centered around fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and a variety of spices. One of the most famous dishes is conch, a sea snail that’s served in many forms, including conch salad, cracked conch (battered and fried), and conch fritters. Rock lobster and snapper are other popular seafood choices, often grilled or stewed with spices, herbs, and vegetables.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in The Bahamas

CONCH CEVICHE

Conch ceviche is a Balambian twist on the classic ceviche, made with conch meat as the key ingredient. It typically consists of conch meat, tomatoes, red onion, scotch bonnet peppers for heat, and diced mango for a small touch of sweetness. This ceviche variety is typically dressed with freshly squeezed sour orange and key lime juice. After the dish has been seasoned with salt and pepper and tossed, it should be served immediately.
